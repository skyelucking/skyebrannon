Buenos Aires - Tips & Trips

 

More than a few people are contemplating a trip to Buenos Aires, so I thought I'd type this handy guide of tips & trips. (I may update it from time to time, so check back.) Enjoy!

Tip: Remember that Buenos Aires is in the Southern Hemisphere. That means toasty Christmases and chilly 4th of July's. Keep that in mind when you're planning. I highly recommend visiting during the warm seasons. From Lonely Planet:

"Best time to visit Buenos Aires is in spring (September through November), when the jacarandas are in bloom and temperatures are blissfully cool, and in fall (March through May). Summer (December through February) in the capital is hot and humid." - Not said: Don't come down in winter!

Tip: Inflation has been a bear over the year we've been here. We've seen prices go up as much as %30-%40. Things are not expensive, but don't expect those famed post-collapse, super-cheap 2001 price tags either. A quick peso to dollar conversion is to divide by 4.  All prices listed are as of 1.24.2011.

Day Trips

Trip:  Horseback Riding: Caballos a la par (website | TripAdvisor Page): We’ve been out with Caballos a la Par twice and had a lovely time both days. (Warning: we were *very* sore the next day, so keep that in mind!)

-          Cost: If booked through an expat friend, the price is $85USD (as of 1.23.11) which includes a ride to the stables.

-          Logistics:  It’s a 30 minute ride outside of BA at a nature preserve, includes some snacks, and lasts from about 9am-6pm.

Trip: Boat Ride: Delta Un-Plugged (website | TripAdvisor Page): We’ve been on the Delta-Unplugged tour 3 times, as it’s first on our list when visitors come. Ana & Ralf are lovely people, the Delta is beautiful, and Ralf’s on boat lunch is delicious. Also, Ana takes pictures of the trip and sends them out promptly afterward.

-          Cost: Price per person u$d80. (drinks included). Public boat is not included, the price is $22.50 (u$d6) return ticket.

-          Logistics:  Take a train from the Retiro station at 7:15am,  1 hour train to Tigre, have a cup of coffee/relax, be at  departure ramp at 9 AM SHARP. The boat trip takes 45 minutes to Delta Unplugged, where they serve you breakfast.  Sail the Tigre through the afternoon, with lunch on the boat, return home (pretty exhausted) around 7.

Trip: Go to Uruguay! (website | TripAdvisor): We’ve been to Montevideo, but that is a 3 hour ferry ride, where Colonia is just an hour’s ferry trip, perfect for a day trip and getting an extra stamp on your passport.  The ferry is nice, like a wide plane, complete with a café, duty free. Accounts from our friends have said the small, quaint city is perfect for a day trip, but not much longer. It’s a cute little town to walk around, eat, and enjoy.

-          Cost/Logistics: Ferry rides leave starting as early as 8:30. Cost ~ 245ARG ($61USD)

 

Tip: Make your own adventure. The city is large enough to offer a wide variety of activities, but

small enough that you can enjoy a number of them in one day if you don't mind taking taxis hither and yon. On the flip side, don’t be afraid of just wandering around one area for a long stretch of time. Palermo offers great little cafes and boutiques and Recoleta offers snazzy shopping options.

 

 

Daytime / Weekday Fun

Japanese Gardens :  (Website |  TripAdvisor )  The Japanese Gardens would only take an hour or so to see, but the restaurant at the gardens is said to have some of the best sushi in the city. So, perhaps a combo of gardens + lunch if you decide to go. Or, you can check out their constant agenda of special events if you’d like to spend more time there. Bring. Bug. Spray.

Zoo Buenos Aires: (Website) I’m not much for zoos, but this is close to the Japanese Gardens and seems to be busy all the time.

Temaiken Park : (Website | TripAdvisor): Again, not much for zoos, but rumor has it that you can get really up close to the animals. I had a friend that kissed a lion and fed a bear by hand there. True story.

Bicycle de Naranja: (Website | Trip Advisor ) This company gives bicycle tours around the city. We haven’t tried this, but friends have said this is a great (and safer than you think) way to explore parts of the city.

-          Cost/Logistics: Monday to Saturday 2pm. AR$ 120 (~30$) the guided bike tour

Shopping in Palermo Soho: I’m not a shopper, but if you are, you can spend a SOLID day,  or afternoon at least,  in Palermo Soho (our neighborhood in fact).  Here are maps of Gastronomy, Clothing, and Interior Design (Art)

Tip: In Palermo Soho, two places that have a solid lunch record are Baraka (a bakery) and La Fabrica (a taco place)

Daytime / Weekday Arty Fun

From its walls, to canvases, to people – Argentines love their aesthetic….

(Fine Art Museum) Museo Nacional de Bellas Artes: (Website | TripAdvisor) It’s free and has an amazing collection of modern Argentine artwork as well as classic works. The museum is walking distance from the Recoleta Cemetary (where Evita is buried) so it might be a good combo for an afternoon.

 - Cost/Logistics: Free. Museum opening times: Tuesday to Friday- 12.30 - 8.30 p.m./ Saturday and Sunday- 9.30 a.m. - 8.30 p.m./Monday closed

Museum of Latin American Art of Buenos Aires (MALBA):  (Website | TripAdvisor): Really enjoyed the collections at the MALBA! It has a nice (albeit overpriced café). Unlike most museums, it’s open on Mondays.

– Cost/Logistics: Museum Hours: Thursday through Monday and Holidays from 12:00 a 8:00./Wednesday till 21:00./ Closed Tuesday. Admission: Adults: $22./ Children under 5: Free.

Graffiti tour:  (Website) I love the fact that, to many Argentine street artists, blank walls are just waiting canvases in this city. We have done an un-official graffiti tour with friends, but this company offers a professional one.  

– Cost/Logistics: When: Group tours run every Wednesday, Thursday, Friday & Saturday. Private tours can be arranged any day (subject to guide availability). Time: Start to finish: 4pm – 7pm approx. Cost: AR$90 pesos per person- includes minibus transport and a list of recommendations.

Choose your own adventure - Art Gallery Edition: There are soooo many wonderful little galleries all over this city. This website has a slick, interactive tool to help you plot out a gallery tour of your own! http://www.mapadelasartes.com/mapa
